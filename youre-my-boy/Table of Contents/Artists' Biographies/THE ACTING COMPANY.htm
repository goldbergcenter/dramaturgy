The Acting Company

 

 

 

Steven Black* (Sherman Adams) spent 15 years working as a profession actor, director and stage manager in New York City ( “As The World Turns”, “The Guiding Light”, and more than seventy films). Since returning to Columbus in 1995, Mr. “B” has performed and/or directed at CATCO, Red Herring, Reality, Arden Shakespeare, and Otterbein College (his alma mater). Steve is the Director of Theatre for the Fort Hayes Career Center. 

 

Jon Farris* (Thomas Dewey) has appeared in a variety of roles at CATCO over the past few years, including conductor Wilhelm Furtwangler in Taking Sides, Andrew Wyke in Sleuth, Richard Nixon in Nixon’s Nixon, Lady Bracknell in The Importance Of Being Earnest, and a double handful of characters in A Tuna Christmas.  Earlier this year, he was featured in Indiana Repertory Theatre's production of Painting Churches.  Mr. Farris is looking forward to joining CATCO audiences on another trip to Tuna, Texas, next June in Greater Tuna. 

 

Chuck Gillespie** (Dwight D. Eisenhower)  After a successful broadcasting career spanning 33 years, Chuck prodly debuts on the CATCO stage.  Throughout the years he performed in dinner theatre and summer stock across the country. Favorite roles include Eddie Carbone in A Veiw From the Bridge, Sir Thomas More in A Man for All Seasons, Big Daddy in Cat on a Hot Tin Roof, Oberon in A Midsummer Night’s Dream, and Fredrik Engerman in A Little Night Music.  A Hilliard resident, Chuck is now Director of Corporate Relations for the American Cancer Society.

 

Mark Mann** (Murray Chotiner) is pleased to be returning to CATCO in the role of Murray Chotiner. CATCO patrons may remember him from such recent offerings as Uncle Vanya (Telegin), Escanaba in Da Moonlight ( Jimmer), Proof ( Robert), and The Grapes of Wrath ( Uncle John). He also performed in the Shorts Festival 2002, and was awarded a GCAC Playwriting Fellowship for his contribution to the Shorts Festival 2004, entitled The Orgy in 414. Mark directed Actor's Theatre's She Stoops To Conquer in August. Mark is Education Director for Columbus Children's Theatre, where he will direct Holes in late winter 2006. He lives in Grove City with his dearest friend (and wife) Danielle.

 

Jonathan Putnam* (Richard M. Nixon) has been performing at CATCO for almost twenty years. Later this season he will take on half the population of Tuna, Texas, in Greater Tuna and will direct the next production here at CATCO, Bad Dates. He is not a crook.

 

Marianne Timmons* (Pat Nixon) is thrilled to be performing in You’re My Boy.  Formerly CATCO’s resident actor and casting director, her credits include Arsenic and Old Lace, The Importance of Being Earnest, Lobby Hero, The Anger in Ernest and Ernestine, The Grapes of Wrath, The Big Slam, and The Fantasticks.  She thanks family and friends, CATCO (including all its loyal patrons), Khufu and Don (her dog and cat), and Chris for all their love and support.

 

 

* Indicates member of AEA

** Indicates member of EMC

 

 

Actors Equity Association was founded in 1913 to protect actors from the severe mistreatment that permeated the industry at that time. Since its inception Equity has had only twelve presidents. The current president is Patrick Quinn. The 35,000-member association consists of distinguished stars and other professional actors and stage managers who work nationwide: from New York's Broadway to Los Angeles, from Minneapolis to Miami Beach, in regional, small professional, stock and dinner theatres, and in theatres for young audiences which build the audiences of tomorrow. These actors and stage managers are committed to working in the theatre as a profession, not an avocation, and bring to you the finest professional training and experience. By presenting Equity productions, this theatre offers you, the audience, the best entertainment presented by the finest quality actors and stage managers that your admission dollars can buy.
