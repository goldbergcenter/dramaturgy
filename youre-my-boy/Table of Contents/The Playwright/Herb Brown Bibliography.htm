The Playwright:  Herb Brown

 

The playwright, Herb Brown, is the author of two novels: Presumption of Guilt (1991, 1992 paperback), which rose to number 10 on the Doubleday national list, and Shadows Of Doubt (1994), which became a selection of The Literary Guild.  His short play, Power Of God, won a competition and was produced by CATCO in its Shorts Festival 2002.  He became fascinated by the relationship between Richard Nixon and Dwight Eisenhower, which reveals much about two who shaped the political landscape of the United States and the world during the middle-to-late 20th century.   You're My Boy is the result of five years of research and is Brown’s first full-length play.

 

Brown received his BA from Denison University and his JD from the University of Michigan Law School, where he served as an Assistant Editor of The Michigan Law Review.  He has taken studies in Shakespeare at Cambridge University in England.  He was a trial lawyer and partner in the firm of Vorys, Sater, Seymour and Pease before being elected a Justice of the Ohio Supreme Court in 1986, where he remained until 1993.  Brown has also served as an arbitrator in complex litigation cases, served the U.S. District Court as Land Commissioner, and was in the Defense Appellate Division of the Judge Advocate General’s Corps.  He has served on a number of boards and commissions, including citizens’ groups to oversee judicial elections and the conduct of other public officials.

 

He was Adjunct Professor of Law at The Ohio State University from 1997-2000, a Bar Examiner for the State of Ohio, and continues to be a frequent lecturer on legal topics.  A fellow in the American College of Trial Lawyers, he was counsel in the libel case of Guccione vs. Hustler Magazine (1980), which resulted in a verdict of $39 million.
