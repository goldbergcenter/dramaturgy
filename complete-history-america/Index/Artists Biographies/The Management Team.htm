The Management Team

 

Every quality theatrical production relies on the Management Team to keep everyone of the artists focused, on-task, and on-time! 

 

The Production Manager is in charge of planning and contracts and ensuring deadlines are met.  S/he also monitors the quality of the production elements.. 

The Stage Manager controls the rehearsals, making note of the Director’s decisions (blocking, entrances/exits),supervises the backstage operations (prop management and dressers), and “calls the show” (cues for sound and lights).  The Stage Manager also enforces Equity rules about rehearsal and performance times.

The Resident Stage Manager usually does what the Stage Manager does, but for this production CATCO is allowing its intern to gain professional experience.  The Resident Stage Manager will supervise rehearsals and monitor performances after opening for quality control. 

 

 

   T. J. Gerckens is in his ninth season as CATCO’s Production Manager. Columbus Business First Magazine named him one of Columbus’ “Top Forty Under Forty” for professional achievement, recognition, and community service. As a lighting designer he has designed many CATCO shows including The Piano Lesson, Bad Dates, Boston Marriage, The Shorts Festival, Grapes of Wrath, and Below the Belt. His design for Metamorphoses on Broadway (directed by Mary Zimmerman) earned a Drama Desk Award in 2002, as well as awards in Chicago, L.A., and San Francisco.  TJ’s lighting designs have been seen at The NY Shakespeare Festival, Lincoln Center SeriousFun! Festival, Actors Theatre of Louisville, Goodman Theatre, and The Mark Taper Forum among others. He received the Jefferson Award for The Odyssey (Chicago) and was nominated for another Drama Desk Award in 2004 for The Notebooks of Leonardo Da Vinci (both directed by Zimmerman).  He holds degrees from Otterbein College and Boston University, and is happily married to scenic designer Stephanie Gerckens.

Deborah S. Singer (Resident Stage Manager) A Columbus native, Deborah has worked at CATCO in different capacities, including a season on Fellowship, since 2000.    She’s been in her current full-time position, stage managing all of CATCO’s shows, since December 2003.   She is most proud of her work on The Grapes of Wrath, The Exonerated, The Complete Works of William Shakespeare (Abridged), and You’re My Boy.    Before she joined the CATCO family, Deb directed a college production of Little Women, did street marketing at the Edinburgh Fringe Festival, served as a leather-clad rocker’s dresser for Saucy Jack and the Space Vixens (also at the Edinburgh Fringe Festival), was an original front-of-house member when Disney’s The Lion King opened on London’s West End, and helped bring the Bard to the masses with Columbus’ The Actors’ Theatre in Schiller Park.    She holds a Bachelor of Arts in Theatre from Otterbein College.
