The Creative Team

 

Geoffrey Nelson (Director; CATCO’s Founder and Artistic Director) was an undergraduate history major at Bowdoin College and is happy to base this production of The Complete History of America [Abridged] on his old lecture notes (or at least the ones he can still decipher).  Nelson studied the Colonial period, the New Deal and the Cold War extensively and pretty much ignored the rest (the Civil War class met too early in the morning so he watched the TV miniseries instead).  Geoff also took an entire class on the Magna Carta, but has yet to find any practical theatrical application for that knowledge (Andrew Lloyd Webber's musical adaptation is still in the early planning stages).  Although he has never made history himself, Geoff's ancestor John Howland is remembered for falling off the Mayflower on its first voyage to America. 

 

 

Stephanie Gerckens (Scenic Designer) CATCO designs include Cat on a Hot Tin Roof, Boston Marriage, Shorts Festival, Escanaba in da Moonlight, Lobby Hero, Murder in Green Meadows, Taste of Sunrise, Fantasticks!, Below the Belt, Forever Plaid.  She has produced other designs for BalletMet (Alice in Wonderland), Otterbein, Northwestern School of Opera, Phoenix, La Comedia, Chicago’s Remains Theatre, Griffin Theatre, Pegasus Players, and Circa-21.  She has been Assistant Scene Designer and Charge Artist at Actors Theatre of Louisville and Scenic artist at BalletMet, Opera Columbus.  She has worked on the feature film Lawn Dogs and at scenic studios in Boston, Washington D.C., and Chicago.  When not designing, Stephanie manages three boys—Max, Ross, and TJ.

 

Kate Robbins (Costume Designer) is pleased to have the opportunity to work with CATCO again.  Past projects include Marvin's Room, The Perfect Ganesh, Flyin' West, The Turn of the Screw and Having Our Say.  Kate has been the Resident Costume Designer and Professor of Theatre at Otterbein College for the past 20 years where she has designed over 80 productions.  She has also captained the intern program, which has led to a 100 percent placement-rate for Otterbein design graduates.  Before coming to Ohio, Kate spent time as an assistant professor and costume designer at East Texas State University and Stephen F. Austin State University (Texas).  She has an MFA in Theatre Design from Humboldt State University and a BA from San Jose State University, both in California.  At the end of this academic year, Kate is retiring at a very young age and returning to Texas.

                                                                       

 

 

John Bohuslawsky (Lighting Designer) most recently designed lights for Pam Tanowitz Dance Company. For BalletMet he lit the touring production of The Nutcracker in Buffalo as well as the Ohio Theatre production, then Rendezvous @ the Riffe in February and Whodunit? in March. Back home in Athens, Ohio, John served as the Production Manager /Resident Lighting Designer for the American College Dance Festival East Central Region. He also resumed his interest in choreography by premiering a work in the Ohio University School of Dance Winter Dance Concert.

Keya Myers-Alkire (Master Electrician/Sound Engineer) grew up in Athens, Ohio, and later attended Ohio University, where she earned her BFA in Theatre. Before joining the CATCO staff as Master Electrician and Sound Engineer in 1997, Keya worked at theaters in Maryland, Utah, and Columbus. Favorite sound designs of hers at CATCO include The Anger in Ernest and Ernestine, A Tuna Christmas, and Escanaba in da Moonlight. She has been married to her wonderful husband, David, for 10 years and they recently welcomed their adorable son, Dean, into the world.

  

 

Bill Childs (Dramaturg) has been dramaturg for CATCO’s The Grapes of Wrath (2003) and You’re My Boy (2005); a member of CATCO’s Board of Trustees (1993-2000, 2003-present); president of the Board (1998-2000); and a dresser for the first five of CATCO’s productions of A Tuna Christmas and Shorts Festival 2000.  He is a professor of History at OSU, teaching Modern America, 20th Century World, and Business History courses.

 

 

Elizabeth Langford (Properties Master) is very excited to be working on this production. Favorite CATCO productions she has propped include Cat on a Hot Tin Roof, The Little Foxes, The Last Night of Ballyhoo, The Grapes of Wrath, and Murder in Green Meadows. Liz has also worked with Senior Repertory of Ohio, Columbus Children’s Theatre, Little Theatre Off Broadway, OSU Opera/Music Theatre Department, and Players Theatre. In her “other life,” Liz works at OSU for International Studies and currently resides in Grandview with her cat Phoebe.  

 

Tatjana Longerot (Costume Studio Manager) was raised in Germany and moved to the USA to study theatre. She earned her MFA in Costume Design from The Ohio State University and received two USITT Peggy Ezekiel Awards for excellent and outstanding achievement in theatre arts. Costume designs for CATCO include Sleuth, Murder in Green Meadows, The Laramie Project, The Exonerated, Lady Day at Emerson’s Bar and Grill, and The Weir. This season she has also designed costumes for OSU Opera, Denison University and The Columbus Academy.

 

 

Leana Faith Wanner (Costume Fellow) would like to thank CATCO for the honor of participating in this past season.  She worked on wardrobe crew for Ghostlight: The Shorts Festival 2006; was a make-up artist for Seascape, and assistant costume designer and sound board operator for The Piano Lesson. Leana graduated from Alma College with a B.A. in Dramaturgy. Her costume designs include Philadelphia and Token to the Moon. She was a Costume Assistant at the SSTC for Dead Guilty.

 

Robin Gordon (Artistic Administrator) holds a Master of Fine Arts degree in Acting and New Works Creation from The Ohio State University. She joined CATCO as an actor in 1999 and has been on staff since 2002. Also an educator, Robin has taught courses at Otterbein College, The Wellington School, Clark State College, University of Toledo, and The Ohio State University.  Robin’s last two performances at CATCO were Maggie in Cat on a Hot Tin Roof and a lizard in Seascape.
