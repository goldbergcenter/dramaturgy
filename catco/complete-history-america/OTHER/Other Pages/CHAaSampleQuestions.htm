Same Questions with answers and/or sources included:

 

 

What was the Society of Cincinnati?

 

http://www.hereditary.us/cin_history.htm  The playwrights are incorrect; the society did not endorse an hereditary president.  It was a society of military officers dedicated to supporting their families through pensions and promoting “service” to the nation.

 

 

What is the difference between the Articles of Confederation and the Federalists Papers?

 

http://www.eagleton.rutgers.edu/e-gov/e-politicalarchive-Constitution.htm

 

 

What does E Pluribus Unum mean? “Out of many, one.”

 

 

What was the “era of Good  Feelings”?

 

What was the Monroe Doctrine?     [“Marilyn could sleep with any of the Kennedy brothers.”  From DVD interview.]

 

http://www.eagleton.rutgers.edu/e-gov/e-politicalarchive-goodfeeling.htm

 

 

Which two presidents died on the same day? Thomas Jefferson and John Adams, July 4, 1826.

 

What was the Know-Nothing Party?

 

http://www.ohiohistorycentral.org/entry.php?rec=911

 

 

What did the 19th Amendment do?  Adopted in 1920, this amendment gave women the right to vote across the U.S. (Some western states had given women some voting rights locally in the late 19th century.)

 

In whose administration was Elvis Presley a "Federal Agent-at-Large" in the Bureau of Narcotics and Dangerous Drugs?

 

None.  Presley asked Richard Nixon to make him a special agent, but the president did not do so (Dec. 1970).  See http://www.gwu.edu/~nsarchiv/nsa/elvis/elnix.html
